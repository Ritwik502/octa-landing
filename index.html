About the Zoo

The Jim Corbett National Park Zoo is a magnificent wildlife sanctuary located in the heart of Jim Corbett National Park. It has a rich history that dates back to its establishment in 1936. The zoo aims to conserve and protect the diverse animal species found in the region, providing visitors with an opportunity to witness the beauty of nature up close.

The zoo is home to a wide range of animals, including majestic tigers, graceful elephants, playful monkeys, and many more. It serves as a habitat for various endangered species and plays a crucial role in their conservation efforts. The zoo's dedicated team of experts and caretakers ensures the well-being and preservation of these animals.

The Jim Corbett National Park Zoo holds a special place in literary history as well. It served as an inspiration for Rudyard Kipling's famous novel, The Jungle Book. The lush forests and diverse wildlife found in the zoo and its surrounding areas were the backdrop for the captivating story that continues to captivate readers worldwide.
